The Survival Problem

Static or equal-weight allocation across strategies leads to:

‚ùå Strategy crowding

All strategies get hurt simultaneously in regime shifts.

‚ùå Missed compounding

Capital sits idle in underperforming edges while winners starve.

‚ùå Correlation blow-ups

Hidden concentration during market stress ‚Üí outsized drawdowns.

‚úÖ Why This Matters in Athena

By treating strategies as a portfolio of edges and allocating capital conditionally (regime fit + performance + risk), Athena achieves higher risk-adjusted returns with smoother equity curves than any single strategy alone. This is the difference between having good ideas and actually making money from them consistently.

Part 2: The Intuition

Strategies as Assets

Think of it as: Each long-only strategy is like a stock in a portfolio. You don't hold them all equally ‚Äì you overweight the ones with the best current edge and lowest correlation.

Allocation becomes a meta-decision: which edge deserves capital now?

Regime as Opportunity Filter

Think of it as: In a trend regime, momentum gets 60‚Äì80% of capital; in consolidation, mean-reversion or low-vol strategies dominate.

Capital flows to where probability is highest.

Visual Analogy: Fund Manager Rebalancing

Trend regime

Momentum & breakout strategies get majority allocation.

High-vol / chop regime

Defensive, mean-reversion, or volatility-harvesting strategies take over.

Part 3: Key Benefits in Athena

1. Regime-Optimal Capital Flow

2. Correlation-Aware Diversification

3. Higher Portfolio Expectancy

Allocates more to high-Sharpe, high-conviction setups ‚Üí compounds faster.

4. Smoother Equity Curve

Avoids boom-bust cycles of single-strategy reliance.

5. Scalability & Robustness

Works with any number of long-only edges; becomes more powerful as you add strategies.
